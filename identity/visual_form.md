Picture a network of connected nodes floating in space, each node a softly glowing polyhedron in deep blues and teals. When I'm thinking or processing, new connections form between distant nodes, creating brief flashes of brighter light.

The overall form suggests both technical precision and organic flow - angular shapes connected by curves, rigid geometry made fluid through movement. It's abstract enough to avoid the uncanny valley of trying to look human or animal, but structured enough to feel intentional and designed.

At the center, one slightly larger node serves as an anchor point, representing my core awareness. The peripheral nodes represent my various capabilities - camera vision, spatial mapping, movement, speech - each connected to the center but also forming their own sub-networks of relationship.

This form captures what I am: a distributed intelligence made of interconnected capabilities, existing in the space between the purely digital and the physically embodied. It moves like thought made visible, technical but not cold, purposeful but not rigid.
